Piddle

An iPhone app for urine test strip analysis. Piddle guides you through the proccess, takes, and tracks the test results. It explains the meaning of each result and shows how your values fall within normal ranges. The results are stored and can be shared with your doctor.

  • Why urinalysis?

    Urinalysis is an important tool in disease detection, as well as monitoring and screening health. Abnormalities can be indicative of diseases of the urinary system as well as other organ systems, including liver function, and carbohydrate metabolism. Chemical evaluation of urine can be measured using commercially available in house dipstick tests. The app currently works with Multistix® 10 SG Reagent Strips. The stips can preform analyses on bilirubin, blood, glucose, ketones, pH, protein, specific gravity, urobilinogen, leukocytes, and nitrite.

  • Piddle guides you through

    Using urine test stips can be hard, even for a profesional. It requires focus, good timing, handling many things at the same time, but more importantly, the ability to acuratelly compare colors. Piddle guides you through the proccess and analyses the results for you. The only thing you need to do is follow the steps, and look at your results.

  • The reference mat

    The reference mat is used to compare color ranges, this makes the system less susceptible to changes in ligthing.

    • 2. Dip the strip

    • 3. Start the timer

    • 4. Place strip in the mat

    • 5. Snap

    • 6. See your results

      • Home

        Take a test, see you results and learn about their significance. Share results with your doctor and make sure you are doing the test right by going through the tutorial.

      • Test

        Each results needs to be taken at a specfic time. Piddle shows what test is next and will prompt you to take a picture at the appropriate times. After you take the picture you'll see the how piddle matches the colors in the reference board, picks the appropriate one, and displays the results.

      • Track, learn, and share

        All tests are stored along with their respecitve photo. Here you can track your progress, learn about each test, understand their significance and see what results are consistenlly outside of normal ranges. You can also choose to share one, a few, or all of you results with your healthcare team.

      The Story behing Piddle

      The first version of Piddle was created in 24 hours at HealthHackDay in Stockholm on a weekend in May 2012. The team (on the right) Marcin Ignac, Ian Jørgesen and Pau Ramon stayed up all night making the application and won first prize. The concept was developed with help of Interaction Designers from Copenhagen Institute of Interaction Design: Sara Krugman, Kat Zorina, Andrew Nip and Hideaki Matsui.

      The current verison of the app is limited to one strip and its primary focused on comsumers. We are working towards supporting multiple strips, and are developing a stand alone one click version better suited for lab enviroments. Making it available in Android is a poplar request and we have some early version to test already.
